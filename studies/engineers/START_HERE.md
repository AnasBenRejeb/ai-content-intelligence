## Learning Path

### Phase 1: Foundations (Week 1-2)

#### 1. Python Advanced Concepts
- **[Real Python - Advanced Python](https://realpython.com/)**
  - Type hints and mypy
  - Decorators and context managers
  - Async/await patterns
  - **Duration**: 10 hours
  - **Priority**: High

#### 2. Design Patterns
- **[Refactoring Guru - Design Patterns](https://refactoring.guru/design-patterns)**
  - Observer pattern (our agents use this)
  - Strategy pattern (our optimization uses this)
  - Template method (BaseAgent uses this)
  - **Duration**: 8 hours
  - **Priority**: High

#### 3. Clean Code Principles
- **Book: "Clean Code" by Robert C. Martin**
  - Meaningful names
  - Functions and classes
  - Error handling
  - **Duration**: 20 hours
  - **Priority**: Medium

### Phase 2: AI/ML Fundamentals (Week 3-4)

#### 1. Natural Language Processing
- **[Stanford CS224N - NLP with Deep Learning](http://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224n/)**
  - Word embeddings
  - Transformers
  - Attention mechanisms
  - **Duration**: 40 hours
  - **Priority**: High

#### 2. Large Language Models
- **[Hugging Face Course](https://huggingface.co/course)**
  - Transformer architecture
  - Fine-tuning models
  - Inference optimization
  - **Duration**: 15 hours
  - **Priority**: High

#### 3. Prompt Engineering
- **[OpenAI Prompt Engineering Guide](https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering)**
  - Effective prompts
  - Few-shot learning
  - Chain-of-thought
  - **Duration**: 5 hours
  - **Priority**: Medium

### Phase 3: System Architecture (Week 5-6)

#### 1. Microservices Architecture
- **[Martin Fowler - Microservices](https://martinfowler.com/articles/microservices.html)**
  - Service decomposition
  - Communication patterns
  - Data management
  - **Duration**: 10 hours
  - **Priority**: Medium

#### 2. Event-Driven Architecture
- **[AWS - Event-Driven Architecture](https://aws.amazon.com/event-driven-architecture/)**
  - Event sourcing
  - CQRS pattern
  - Message queues
  - **Duration**: 8 hours
  - **Priority**: Low

#### 3. Scalability Patterns
- **[High Scalability Blog](http://highscalability.com/)**
  - Caching strategies
  - Load balancing
  - Database sharding
  - **Duration**: 12 hours
  - **Priority**: Medium

### Phase 4: Testing & Quality (Week 7-8)

#### 1. Test-Driven Development
- **[Test-Driven Development by Example](https://www.amazon.com/Test-Driven-Development-Kent-Beck/dp/0321146530)**
  - Red-Green-Refactor
  - Unit testing best practices
  - Mocking and stubbing
  - **Duration**: 15 hours
  - **Priority**: High

#### 2. Integration Testing
- **[Martin Fowler - Testing Strategies](https://martinfowler.com/articles/practical-test-pyramid.html)**
  - Test pyramid
  - Integration patterns
  - E2E testing
  - **Duration**: 8 hours
  - **Priority**: Medium

## Essential Reading

### Books

1. **"Clean Code" by Robert C. Martin**
   - Why: Foundation of good coding practices
   - Time: 20 hours
   - Priority: Must-read

2. **"Design Patterns" by Gang of Four**
   - Why: Understand patterns we use
   - Time: 30 hours
   - Priority: Should-read

3. **"The Pragmatic Programmer" by Hunt & Thomas**
   - Why: Professional development mindset
   - Time: 15 hours
   - Priority: Should-read

### Papers

1. **"Attention Is All You Need" (Transformer paper)**
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762
   - Why: Foundation of modern LLMs
   - Time: 3 hours
   - Priority: Must-read

2. **"BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers"**
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805
   - Why: Understanding contextual embeddings
   - Time: 2 hours
   - Priority: Should-read
